About Me

I first started my undergraduate coursework at Hunter College in psychology and biological sciences. I had immigrated to the United States a year before to pursue my higher education and explore work opportunities that were not available to women in Iran. In my third year as a student here, I enrolled in a computer science class to satisfy my general education requirements. As a consequence I quickly became fascinated with the subject. I was drawn to how much I could relate my observations about the world around me with the concepts I studied in this class. In that first class, I met the man who later became my husband. He is a very successful Software Engineer and works for a startup company now as a full stack engineer. His special passion for computer coding and computer science classes excited and motivated me intellectualy and personaly.

As someone who went through two major envirenmental changes, first from Iran to Germany and then Germany to the United States, I believe that I started to realise the important of technology.
